What I specialise in

I believe that a child's native language is inseparable from their identity. I teach Russian at first-language level to heritage speakers — helping children stay connected to their families and culture, wherever they grow up.

I am equally experienced with students learning Russian as a foreign language, adapting methodology across age groups and proficiency levels — from playful early-years immersion to structured RFL certification exam preparation. This dual specialisation means I can serve the full range of learners in a Russian programme.

Pedagogy through science

Evidence-based teaching

I don't choose methods because they feel intuitive — I choose them because research supports them. As a Research Assistant at the Laboratory of Cognitive and Linguistic Studies and an M.A. candidate in Applied Linguistics, I read current studies and apply their findings directly to lesson design.

This matters especially for heritage and bilingual learners, where the research on language maintenance, identity, and acquisition is rich — and where poor methodology can quietly undermine a child's relationship with their native tongue.

I am actively exploring AI tools in language education — how they can personalise feedback, support independent practice, and extend learning beyond the classroom. And I contribute to the expert review of a bilingual children's textbook, bridging research and real curriculum materials.

A story from the classroom

Believing in every student

"I never doubted he could do it. That belief turned out to matter more than anything."

In Colombia, I was preparing students for official RFL certification exams. One student — Gabriel — was, by every measure, below the threshold for A1. My colleagues advised redirecting him to an ungraded sitting.

I understood their caution. But I saw something the numbers didn't capture. So I proposed a middle ground: a dual registration — graded and ungraded — so he'd walk away with something regardless.

Then I got to work. Extra sessions, free of charge. Materials tailored to his gaps. Voice recordings back and forth to track his progress week by week.

When the results arrived, he had passed. Certificate in hand. That moment crystallised something I now hold as a core belief: a student's achievement is inseparable from their teacher's faith in them.

Gabriel, I am so proud of you!

Teaching philosophy

How I teach

"A child who keeps their mother tongue keeps a part of who they are."

For children from Russian-speaking families, language is memory, identity, and connection to home. My lessons honour this — combining rigorous linguistic work with literature, storytelling, and play.

With foreign-language learners, I use communicative, task-based, and game-based approaches that make Russian feel alive — a tool to connect with people, not a set of rules to memorise.

Across all groups, I adapt constantly — to age, proficiency, and context. And I never stop learning myself.
